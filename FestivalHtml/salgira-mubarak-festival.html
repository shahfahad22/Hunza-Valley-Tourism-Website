Salgirah Mubarak (Imamat Day) – A Spiritual and Community Celebration

Overview

Salgirah Mubarak is a religious, spiritual, and community-centered festival celebrated in Hunza Valley. It marks the birthday of the Ismaili Muslim spiritual leader, Prince Karim Aga Khan IV. The festival is not only a joyful occasion but also a time to promote unity, gratitude, service, and peace. Since the majority of people in Hunza are Ismaili Muslims, the day holds deep spiritual importance.

Date of Celebration

Celebrated annually on December 13, the birthday of His Highness Prince Karim Aga Khan IV (born in 1936).

Main Celebration Spots

Observed throughout Hunza, particularly in Central Hunza (Aliabad, Karimabad, Altit, Baltit), Upper Hunza (Gulmit, Passu, Gojal), and some parts of Lower Hunza and Ghizer.

Purpose and Spiritual Meaning

  • Thankfulness for spiritual guidance in life
  • Renewal of faith and community unity
  • Encouragement of voluntary service (Khidmat)
  • Promotion of peace, education, and development, as guided by the Imam
  • People dedicate the day to self-improvement and service to humanity

Key Activities and Traditions

  • Special Decorations:
    • Jamat Khanas (community centers) are decorated with lights, banners, and colors
    • Homes and streets are illuminated with lamps and festive lighting
  • Spiritual Gatherings and Prayers:
    • Special prayers and spiritual talks are held at Jamat Khanas in the morning and evening
    • Quranic verses and Imam’s Farmans (guidance) are recited
    • Prayers are offered for personal growth and communal well-being
  • Community Services and Volunteering:
    • Youth and adults engage in clean-up drives, tree planting, food distribution, and assisting the needy
    • Blood donation camps, health check-ups, and educational seminars are also organized
  • Cultural Performances:
    • Children and youth perform religious songs, poetry, and role plays
    • Traditional folk music and dance are included in a modest and spiritual tone
  • Distribution of Sweets and Food:
    • Traditional sweets are shared across the community
    • Special dishes include:
      • Diram Phitti (sweet wheat bread)
      • Khesta (local noodles)
      • Giyaling (honey bread)
      • Fruit salads, tea, and dry fruits
  • Storytelling and Reflection:
    • Community elders share stories of the Imam’s global achievements
    • Topics discussed include:
      • Education
      • Health
      • Peace-building
      • Human unity

Cultural and Social Impact

This festival spiritually connects people of Hunza, promotes brotherhood, and encourages volunteerism. It inspires the youth to contribute to education, a clean environment, and peace-building, in line with the Imam's vision. It also strengthens their cultural identity and traditions.

About Prince Karim Aga Khan IV

Imam of the Shia Imami Ismaili Muslims since 1957, known worldwide for his contributions in:

  • Education: Aga Khan Universities
  • Health: Aga Khan Hospitals
  • Cultural Preservation: Aga Khan Trust for Culture
  • Poverty Alleviation: Aga Khan Development Network (AKDN)

His vision: “Islam is a thinking, spiritual faith that teaches compassion and service.”

Tourist and Outsider Perspective

Although not publicly advertised, tourists visiting Hunza during this time feel the spiritual and communal unity. Locals welcome outsiders with respect, and researchers and peace workers view the festival as a model of community harmony.

Conclusion

Salgirah Mubarak is a deeply meaningful, spiritual, and community-driven celebration in Hunza. It reminds people that their Imam provides spiritual and worldly guidance. The lights, prayers, service, and smiles on this day fill Hunza with a unique positive energy.
